SUPERVISED FSD — ROUTE PLAN

Two FSD Test Routes in Burnaby, BC

Produced by D. Chan in 2026

Two complementary loops designed to exercise a broad mix of road conditions — highway merging, a mountain roundabout, a coastal village drive to Deep Cove, dense pedestrian arterials, residential/school streets, and industrial corridors. Each route is independently capped at under two hours of drive time.

Route 1 — North Burnaby, SFU Mountain & Deep Cove Loop

~1h 45m–1h 55m drive time ~68 km distance Start: Brentwood Town Centre
Route sequence
  1. Brentwood Town CentreStart — dense mall traffic, pick-up/drop-off lanes, multi-lane arterial merges
  2. Hastings St corridorBus lanes, frequent signals, mid-block pedestrian crossings
  3. Burnaby Mountain ParkwayWinding forested climb, tight curves, elevation change
  4. Gaglardi Way → SFUSteep sustained grade, reduced visibility on bends
  5. SFU University Drive WestSingle-lane roundabout at the campus core
  6. Barnet Hwy → Deep Cove, North VancouverDivided highway, then a narrow winding coastal road into the village
  7. Deep Cove villageTight cul-de-sac turnaround, heavy tourist foot traffic, tour-bus parking
  8. Ironworkers Memorial (2nd Narrows) Bridge, Hwy 1High-traffic bridge merge back into Burnaby
  9. Lougheed Hwy → Willingdon AveReturn via multi-lane arterial, protected/unprotected left turns

Notes on FSD version & testing

  • As of late June 2026, HW4/AI4 vehicles run FSD (Supervised) v14.3.4; HW3/AI3 vehicles are receiving the newly launched FSD v14 Lite (build 2026.20.5.1), which distills v14 driving behavior for the older AI3 computer. Confirm your car's installed version under Controls → Software before testing, since rollout is staged by hardware and region.
  • FSD (Supervised) remains a Level 2 driver-assist system — stay attentive, hands ready, and be prepared to take over at any time on every scenario above, especially the mountain grade, roundabout, and highway merges.
  • Drive times are Google Maps estimates for typical mid-day traffic; add buffer during weekday rush hours (7–9am, 3–6pm) when arterials like Kingsway and Hastings St slow considerably.
  • Route order can be reversed or split across two separate outings if you'd prefer shorter individual sessions.
